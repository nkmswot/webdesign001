Common Questions

Frequently asked
questions

Answers to what prospective clients ask us most.

Our minimum for discretionary investment management is $500,000 in investable assets. For financial planning engagements only, we may work with clients below this threshold on a fee-for-service basis.
We are a fee-only fiduciary advisor. We charge an annual percentage of assets under management (AUM), typically 0.75%–1.0%, tiered by asset level. We never receive commissions or third-party compensation of any kind.
As a fiduciary, we are legally and ethically obligated to act in your best interest at all times — not in our own. This is a higher standard than the "suitability" standard many brokers operate under. Always ask your advisor if they are a fiduciary.
Yes. We facilitate ACAT (Automated Customer Account Transfer) transfers from virtually any custodian. We also manage accounts held at Schwab, Fidelity, and TD Ameritrade. Transfers typically complete in 5–7 business days.
We conduct formal reviews at least semi-annually, plus ad hoc meetings whenever your circumstances change (job change, inheritance, divorce, business sale, etc.). You'll also receive quarterly portfolio reports and our market commentary.
Your assets are held at independent third-party custodians (Schwab or Fidelity), not at Meridian. We have discretionary authority to trade but never to withdraw or transfer your funds. Custodial accounts are SIPC-insured up to $500,000.
